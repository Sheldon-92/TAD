# Per-Type Validation Checklist

Use this checklist AFTER /pressure-test verdict. These are the type-specific validation steps before committing to build.

---

## Software Validation Checklist

Before writing a line of code:

- [ ] **Demand**: Found ≥1 Reddit/HN/forum thread where strangers describe this exact problem (not similar — exact)
- [ ] **Competitor audit**: Named the 3 closest competitors, checked their pricing, identified what they don't do
- [ ] **Target persona**: Can name 3 specific people (first name, company, job) who would use this in production
- [ ] **MVP clarity**: Can describe the full MVP in one sentence without the word "and"
- [ ] **Willingness to pay**: At least 1 person has expressed willingness to pay a specific amount (not "yeah, I'd pay for that")
- [ ] **Distribution**: Have a specific plan to reach the first 50 users (not "social media")
- [ ] **Legal scan**: No obvious IP conflicts, API ToS violations, or data privacy issues with the MVP approach
- [ ] **Build time**: MVP is buildable in 2 weeks by the founding team

**Minimum threshold to proceed**: 6 of 8 checked. If <6, run more customer discovery before building.

## Ecommerce Validation Checklist

Before ordering inventory:

- [ ] **Demand**: Found Amazon/Etsy/similar marketplace with 3+ products in category having >100 reviews and BSR < 50,000
- [ ] **Differentiation**: Identified specific gap in top 3 competitor reviews (what they complain about, what they miss)
- [ ] **Unit economics**: COGS + FBA fees + packaging leaves ≥40% gross margin at target retail price
- [ ] **Supplier identified**: Found ≥2 Alibaba suppliers for the product at acceptable MOQ and price
- [ ] **Keyword validated**: Found ≥1 search term with real volume on Amazon/Google that matches this product
- [ ] **Test sell plan**: Have a specific plan to sell first 10-50 units without paid advertising
- [ ] **Risk check**: Verified product is not in a restricted category, doesn't require certification that isn't planned, no IP issues
- [ ] **Listing draft**: Written a draft product title + 3 bullet points that clearly differentiate from competitors

**Minimum threshold to proceed**: 6 of 8 checked. If <6, do more market research before ordering.

## Hardware Validation Checklist

Before spending on tooling or significant manufacturing:

- [ ] **Demand proxy**: Found ≥1 Kickstarter campaign in this category that successfully funded (any time period)
- [ ] **Competitive landscape**: Named 3 existing products, checked their pricing and reviews, identified what buyers complain about
- [ ] **Prototype test**: Built or ordered a rough prototype and gotten reactions from ≥3 non-friends
- [ ] **BOM estimate**: Rough BOM done, unit economics make sense at projected retail price and realistic manufacturing scale
- [ ] **Supplier identified**: Found ≥1 manufacturer for the core component at realistic cost
- [ ] **Crowdfunding feasibility**: Have a rough campaign concept — enough for a landing page test
- [ ] **Regulatory check**: Identified which certifications are required for target market (CE, FCC, FDA, etc.) and estimated cost/timeline
- [ ] **IP check**: No obvious patent conflicts (basic Google Patent search done)

**Minimum threshold to proceed**: 5 of 8 checked. Hardware has higher stakes — don't compromise on BOM + regulatory.

## Service Validation Checklist

Before quitting your job or making commitments:

- [ ] **Market rate**: Checked Upwork/Fiverr/LinkedIn for current rates in this service category — know the market
- [ ] **First client identified**: Have a specific person or company who has expressed interest in paying for this service
- [ ] **Service scope**: Can describe the exact deliverable and timeline for the service in 2 sentences
- [ ] **Competitive positioning**: Know what alternatives clients use and why your service is different
- [ ] **Delivery proof**: Have delivered a version of this service (even free/discounted) and received positive feedback
- [ ] **Pricing test**: Have quoted a price to ≥1 potential client and gotten a response (positive or negotiating)
- [ ] **Referral potential**: Service delivers a specific, describable outcome that clients would tell colleagues about
- [ ] **Systemization vision**: Can describe what this service looks like systematized in 6 months (not just founder-delivered)

**Minimum threshold to proceed**: 5 of 8 checked. Focus especially on first client and pricing test — those two override everything else.

## Content Validation Checklist

Before committing to a content schedule:

- [ ] **Thesis test**: Published ≥1 piece on the core thesis and measured engagement vs. baseline
- [ ] **Audience exists**: Found ≥1 community (subreddit, Facebook group, Discord, newsletter) where target audience is active
- [ ] **Competitor landscape**: Identified the 3 creators who cover closest topic, understood their format and gaps
- [ ] **Differentiation**: Can articulate the one thing that makes this content different from those 3 creators in one sentence
- [ ] **Format-channel fit**: Tested content in the target format (written/video/audio) and confirmed it's sustainable to produce
- [ ] **First 100 path**: Have a specific plan to get the first 100 subscribers/followers that doesn't depend on luck
- [ ] **Monetization model**: Identified ≥2 comparable creators in the niche who monetize, understood their model
- [ ] **Time commitment**: Calculated the weekly time commitment for consistent publishing and confirmed it's sustainable

**Minimum threshold to proceed**: 5 of 8 checked. Thesis test and first 100 path are the most important.

## Marketplace Validation Checklist

Before building the matching/transaction infrastructure:

- [ ] **Both sides identified**: Can name ≥10 people on each side who would participate
- [ ] **Manual transaction**: Completed ≥1 manual transaction between a real supply-side and demand-side participant
- [ ] **Take rate accepted**: At least 1 participant on each side has accepted the proposed take rate
- [ ] **Cold start plan**: Have a specific strategy to build initial supply without demand, and vice versa
- [ ] **Trust mechanism**: Have a plan for how trust is established between strangers on the platform
- [ ] **Incumbent analysis**: Checked if a dominant player already exists and can articulate why this marketplace is different
- [ ] **Unit economics**: At projected GMV and take rate, path to profitability is visible within 18 months
- [ ] **Regulatory check**: Verified that connecting these two parties is legally compliant (especially financial services, professional services, housing)

**Minimum threshold to proceed**: 6 of 8 checked. Manual transaction and both-sides identification are non-negotiable.
