Triangle Agent Development

A methodology for building software with AI agents — two agents, four quality gates, and a human in the loop.

What is TAD?

TAD is a structured methodology for software development where two specialized AI agents collaborate under human supervision. Alex designs solutions and creates handoff documents. Blake implements them through an iterative quality loop. The human makes all critical decisions.

The framework enforces quality through four mandatory gates, evidence-based verification, and terminal isolation between agents. It scales from quick fixes to multi-phase epics.

Key Features

▲

Two-Agent System

Alex (Solution Lead) handles design and requirements. Blake (Execution Master) handles implementation and testing. Neither can do the other's job.

⚫

Four Quality Gates

Gate 1: Requirements clarity. Gate 2: Design completeness. Gate 3: Implementation quality. Gate 4: Business acceptance. Every gate is a checkpoint.

↻

Ralph Loop

Blake's iterative quality cycle: Layer 1 self-checks (build, test, lint), then Layer 2 expert sub-agent reviews. Circuit breaker prevents infinite loops.

💬

Structured Feedback

For non-code artifacts, Blake generates feedback HTML alongside deliverables. Humans review element-by-element and export structured JSON for the next iteration.

How It Works

1

Describe your goal

Tell Alex what you want to build. Alex asks clarifying questions (Socratic inquiry) and researches the problem space before designing a solution.

2

Alex creates a handoff

A detailed document with requirements, technical design, acceptance criteria, and implementation steps. Expert reviewers catch issues before Blake starts.

3

Blake implements

In a separate terminal, Blake reads the handoff and executes through the Ralph Loop — writing code, running tests, and getting expert reviews until everything passes.

4

You accept the result

Alex runs Gate 4 business acceptance. You verify the result meets your intent, not just the letter of the spec. Knowledge is captured for future sessions.
