### 1. Evaluation of REVISED Synthesis
The revised synthesis is a significant improvement in evidentiary grounding. It moves TAD from a "pedagogical tool" (unproven) to an **"operating system for the full-stack era"** (proven).
*   **"Expands operating range"** vs "Human learns": This aligns with the observed "editor-shift." The human isn't necessarily becoming a deep-domain expert; they are becoming a **high-judgment operator**. You aren't learning the language; you are learning to pilot the agent that speaks it.
*   **"Beneficial friction needs pre-existing judgment"**: This is the decisive pivot. It acknowledges that TAD is for the "displaced expert"—someone with senior-level judgment who is operating in a domain where they lack syntax/boilerplate mastery. Without that judgment, the gates are rubber-stamped and the "friction" is wasted.

### 2. Decision-Grade Implication
**CORRECT.** You cannot market "Beneficial Friction" while maintaining a "Mechanical Enforcement Rejected" architecture.
*   Currently, TAD's gates are **advisory**. The agent can (and does) skip Layer 2 reviews or knowledge assessments if "under pressure." 
*   If the differentiator is the *mechanistic* enforcement of the third corner (Residue), the 2026-04-15 decision must be reversed. You need **deterministic, code-enforced validators** that physically block the creation of a COMPLETION report until the schema/evidence requirements are satisfied. Without this, TAD is just a very long system prompt.

### 3. One Remaining Blind Spot: The "Staleness Trap"
The synthesis assumes "residue" is a compounding asset. In high-velocity domains (AI, Frontend, Security), residue has a **half-life**.
*   **The Blind Spot:** TAD lacks a **Decay/Refresh Mechanism**.
*   If a user returns to a project after 6 months, the "cheaper next pass" anchors the agent to stale architecture, deprecated APIs, or solved security vulnerabilities recorded in the residue.
*   **Risk:** The residue becomes a "Hallucination Anchor"—the agent trusts the stale `.tad/project-knowledge` more than the live internet or latest package docs. A true "capability-acquisition methodology" must include **Autonomous Deprecation**: a way to challenge and prune the residue so the "next pass" isn't just cheaper, but *current*.
