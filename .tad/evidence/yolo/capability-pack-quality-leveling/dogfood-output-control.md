# CONTROL Answer (generalist, NO pack)

Scenario: I have 3 portrait photos with different expressions/poses, and I want to make a
6-second beat-synced dynamic video with lofi background music.

## How I'd approach it

Great project! Here's a plan to turn your 3 photos into a snappy 6-second clip:

1. **Pick your music first.** Find a lofi track you like — lofi usually sits around a
   relaxed tempo, so the beats are gentle and evenly spaced. Trim it to 6 seconds.

2. **Tap out the beats.** Listen and mark where the strong beats land. With 3 photos over
   6 seconds you'll roughly show each photo for about 2 seconds, switching on a beat so it
   feels synced ("卡点").

3. **Add some life to each photo.** Static photos look flat, so apply a gentle Ken Burns
   effect — a slow zoom or pan on each image. You can do this in CapCut, Premiere, After
   Effects, or even iMovie. Alternate the direction (zoom in on one, pan on the next) for
   variety.

4. **Use transitions on the cuts.** A quick fade or a soft swipe between photos on each
   beat keeps it smooth. Don't overdo the effects.

5. **Mix the audio.** Just drop the lofi track underneath at a comfortable level. No
   narration needed here, so the music carries it.

6. **Export.** For social media, export vertical 1080x1920 at 30fps, H.264.

Tools: CapCut is the easiest for beat-synced photo videos and has auto-beat detection.
If you want more control, Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve work well too.

That should give you a clean, rhythmic little montage-style edit. Have fun with it!
