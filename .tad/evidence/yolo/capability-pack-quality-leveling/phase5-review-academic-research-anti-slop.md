### Weaknesses / things flagged (do NOT block, but should be logged)
- **ScholarEval provenance blur (P2)**: scholar-eval.md presents an 8-dimension weighted rubric (Rigor 25% / Impact 20% / Novelty 15% / Reproducibility 15% / Clarity 10% / Coherence 10% / Limitations 3% / Ethics 2%) and attributes it to "the ScholarEval framework (Moussa et al., 2025, arXiv:2510.16234)." The actual paper's framework is built on TWO criteria (soundness + contribution) over a 117-idea dataset. The 8-dim weighted rubric in fact originates from ScienceClaw's SCIENCE.md lines 569-583 (also cited). So the rubric is real-and-internally-sourced, but the arXiv attribution implies the paper supplies the 8 dimensions/weights when it does not. The weights themselves (25/20/15/15/10/10/3/2) are the one cluster of numbers with NO external research anchor — they read as ScienceClaw house convention, not derived. Not slop (they're operational and traceable to SCIENCE.md), but the "grounded in arXiv:2510.16234" framing overstates external grounding. Recommend: re-label the weights as ScienceClaw-derived, cite the paper only for the *concept* of literature-grounded evaluation.
- **A few "soft" generic rules survive in the bodies** (NOT masquerading as depth — they're correctly presented as conventions, not pack-unique thresholds): e.g. statistics.md APA "report exact p-value," domain-social.md R-SOC-071 "maintain political neutrality," R-SOC-090 "IRB approval mandatory." These are restatable-by-any-LLM, but they sit in clearly-labeled convention/integrity sections, not in the threshold tables, so they do not inflate specN or dress up as depth. Acceptable.
- **Citation-count classification bands** (literature-search.md L24-29: Noteworthy 20+, Landmark 500+, Seminal 500+, etc.) and **journal IF tiers** are ScienceClaw house heuristics with no external citation. They are specific numbers but arbitrary/unsourced (sourced only to "literature-review SKILL"). Low risk — they're internal editorial conventions, not empirical claims — but they are the closest thing to "unsourced numbers dressed as research." Flag, don't block.
